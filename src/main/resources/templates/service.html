Seed Sales and Distribution

Organic seed suppliers or retailers offer a range of organic seeds for sale. They provide catalogs, online platforms, or physical stores where customers can browse and purchase organic seeds for their gardens, farms, or landscaping projects.

Seed Testing and Certification:

Organic seeds must meet specific standards to be certified as organic. Seed testing services are available to verify the quality and purity of the seeds, ensuring they are free from chemical residues, GMOs, and contamination. Organic certification bodies provide certifications to ensure the seeds meet the required organic standards.

Seed Variety Trials

Research institutions and organizations conduct trials and evaluations of different organic seed varieties to assess their performance in organic farming systems. The results help farmers and growers make informed decisions when selecting appropriate seed varieties for their organic production.

Educational Resources

Organic seed services may include educational materials, workshops, or training programs on organic seed production, seed saving techniques, and organic farming practices. These resources aim to promote knowledge sharing and support individuals or communities interested in organic seed production.

Seed Saving and Exchange

Some organizations and communities promote seed saving and exchange programs. They encourage gardeners and farmers to save seeds from their organic crops, preserving traditional and heirloom varieties. Seed exchange events or platforms facilitate the sharing of organic seeds among enthusiasts to promote diversity and sustainability.
